# UNSEEN-LAYERS
School project  
Plot: The player is an ordinary person desperate to regain their self (personality) within 18 hours, and must balance "time" and "energy" through daily choices such as cooking, ordering takeout, cleaning, and resting. After 18 hours, this ordinary person will die in this mental world; if the mental world collapses, the protagonist will never wake up again.  
Theme (Beneath the Surface): This so-called "apartment" is actually the mental world locked down by the main personality after suffering severe trauma. The player is not the owner, but a newly split "protective/compensatory personality." After 18 hours, the main personality will face "mental brain death." Up until this point, the plot is rather linear.  
After the player gets out of the "apartment", he will find himself on a street instead of back at the real world. He is no longer bounded by that 18-hour limit, and has retrieved full memory. He will have to explore the streets, encounter antagonists and different characters, in order to find out the reality of this world.  
For the first part of the story, time management is not really an issue. And there's only one way of dying in the first part of the story, which is to use up time. However, in the second part of the story, we have created multiple death scenes, in which most of them can be triggered by getting into the boss fight. We also included a fake ending to make the game more interesting. The first part of the game is basically set-ups of the story, while the second part includes more playable parts. The player will have to determine their own paths in order to get into the real ending.  
All images (except for the weird car) are AI-generated (by Gemini). I spent hours tormenting myself by making Gemini get me somehting I want.  
The demonic sword and the boss (including boss fight music) are from the Devil May Cry series by Capcom. I am personally a fan of the game series, so I put Dante from DMC4 as the most powerful character in the entire game.  
But I can guarentee that ALL plot and lines are written by us, purely HB-generated (Human Brain generated), except for the riddles provided by the stationary store owner in the second part of the story. We've spent hours and hours deciding on our plot and the lines.  
Try to explore and choose as much options as possible. I planted some ester eggs inside.  
To play them, simply download them, unzip them, and double-click the exe file or .dmg file to play  
Have fun!

SPOILER ALERT:  
Full plot of the second part of the game:  
  
The protagonist steps out of the room and finds themselves on a main street. The street has a pharmacy, a dungeon entrance, a pizza shop, a stationery shop, and a black mist in the distance.  
  
The moment the protagonist leaves the room, they merge with the "???" that had been guiding them: the protagonist is the dominant personality in control of the body, while "???" is a fragment of the protagonist's memories. "???" had been guiding the protagonist out of that room all along. However, strangely, after arriving on the main street, a new voice of "???" appears in the protagonist's mind (hereinafter referred to as P2). P2 tempts the protagonist to pass through the black mist at the end of the street, arrive at the Land's End, end everything, and wake up quickly.  
  
**Pizza Shop:** Players can buy pizza or a pizza + strawberry sundae combo. The strawberry sundae must be eaten immediately because it melts quickly, while the pizza can either be taken away or eaten on the spot. The protagonist has no money at the start and cannot buy anything. The pizza shop owner hints that the protagonist can explore the dungeon (though it is extremely dangerous) or find the stationery shop (bookstore) owner to play some mini-games. The bookstore owner may give the protagonist some rewards.  
  
**Pharmacy:** Players can buy a "potion that can conceal one's presence."  
  
**The Black Mist at the End of the Street:** If the protagonist insists on entering, a boss battle will be triggered, and the protagonist will fight against a man in red. However, no matter what, the protagonist will be killed. The man in red will give the protagonist three chances to give up. If the protagonist still chooses to attack the man in red after three rounds, the man in red will execute the protagonist by gunshot. If the protagonist listens to P2 and verbally attacks the man in red, the man in red will angrily kill the protagonist with a single sword strike, then say, "Sorry, pal. Looks like you're beyond saving." If the protagonist stabs the man in red with the old sword found in the hardware store, they will find that it cannot pierce his defense at all. If the protagonist attacks the man in red with the Yamato (mentioned later), although the sword goes right through his chest, he will act as if nothing happened, still smiling as he carries his greatsword. The protagonist, terrified, will fall to the ground and be executed.  
  
If the protagonist uses the "potion that can conceal one's presence" before triggering the boss battle, they can bypass Dante (the man in red) and reach an altar. If the protagonist decides to turn back and return the way they came after reaching the altar, the potion's effect will have already worn off, and Dante will kill them. If the protagonist has obtained the Yamato (the method of obtaining it will be mentioned later), P2 will urge the protagonist to thrust the Yamato into the altar, claiming it is "the solution to everything." However, in reality, the altar collapses, and the protagonist falls down along with it. On the verge of death, the protagonist hears that P2 is actually a second personality born when the protagonist was on the brink of brain death. P2's goal is to trick the protagonist into destroying the altar (which is the core of the protagonist's spiritual world), so that P2 can take over the body and usurp the dominant personality. This unlocks the False Ending.  
  
**Stationery Shop:** The protagonist can play a riddle game with the shop owner. Guessing three in a row correctly will yield a monetary reward. After playing the first round, if the protagonist buys a pizza for the stationery shop owner (the owner hints that they will give a map), the owner will provide some information about the dungeon as a reward. They will say a string of letters, hinting that it is the key to clearing the dungeon. The owner says there is a demonic sword at the end of the dungeon, and if the protagonist brings it back, the owner will give the protagonist a book. After completing this task, the owner gives the protagonist an old book. The book mentions the twin sons born of the demon swordsman Sparda and the human Eva. The elder son, Vergil, prefers using a katana and has a cold personality. The demonic sword at the end of the dungeon is Vergil's weapon, the Yamato. The younger son, Dante, loves using a greatsword and enjoys eating pizza. From this, it can be deduced that the man in red guarding the end of the mist is Dante. The book mentions that the Yamato has the property of "separation" and can cut through space and time. Vergil once used it to open a gate to the demon world and also to separate his demonic and human aspects. This sword is capable of wounding Dante.  
  
**How to Unlock the True Ending:** After obtaining the Yamato, go find Dante, making sure to have at least one pizza in the inventory. Eat the pizza in front of the mist, and Dante will be drawn over. He tells the protagonist that although he shouldn't be doing this, if the protagonist buys him a pizza and a strawberry sundae, he will help. At this moment, P2 will try desperately to persuade the protagonist, telling them that Dante is a madman who will only kill them. Meanwhile, Dante tells the protagonist that they are "possessed," and that if they want to resolve the anomaly within them, Dante's demonic bloodline is essential. Only by resolving the anomaly can the protagonist return to the real world. If the protagonist refuses Dante, Dante will return to the mist and continue his guard duty, and the protagonist will need to buy another pizza to lure Dante out again. If the protagonist agrees, they will bring Dante to the pizza shop. If the protagonist doesn't have enough money for the pizza + strawberry sundae combo, Dante will wait at the restaurant, and the protagonist will need to go to the stationery shop owner to complete a second riddle game (anti-softlock design).  
  
If the protagonist does not return to Dante but instead takes the opportunity of his absence to go to the altar, a heated argument will erupt between the protagonist and P2. P2 will say that Dante is a madman, while the protagonist will begin to doubt P2's intentions from the very beginning. If the protagonist tries to use the Yamato to separate themselves from P2, they will die from excessive blood loss because, as a human, they cannot activate the Yamato's power.  
  
If the Player chooses to return to the pizza shop, after buying the strawberry sundae and pizza for Dante, Dante will accompany the protagonist through the mist to the altar. Dante tells the protagonist to trust him. All along, Dante has been searching for P2 within the protagonist, attempting to kill P2. It was Dante who had been guarding the core part of the protagonist's personality (the altar). At the altar, Dante plunges the Yamato into the protagonist's chest, separating the protagonist from P2. At that moment, a blinding white light bursts from the altar, and the protagonist's vision goes white before they wake up back on a hospital bed in the real world.
